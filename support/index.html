Setting up the keyboard

VoiceDrop ships with a keyboard extension that lets you dictate voice-to-text from inside any messaging app. Setup is a one-time thing:

  • iOS Settings → General → Keyboard → Keyboards → Add New Keyboard → select VoiceDrop.
  • Tap the newly-added VoiceDrop row → toggle Allow Full Access on.
  • In any app that accepts text, tap the globe key (🌐) on the keyboard → switch to VoiceDrop.
  • Tap the big red mic button. VoiceDrop opens, you dictate, silence auto-stops the recording.
  • Read the transcript. When satisfied, tap the ‹ [App Name] breadcrumb in the iOS top-left corner. You're back in your messaging app with the transcript inserted at your cursor.

Frequently asked

Why does the keyboard need Full Access?

The keyboard needs to read the transcribed text from a shared App Group storage slot — that's the only reason. It does not transmit keystrokes, record audio, make network requests, or include any analytics. Audio recording and transcription happen exclusively in the main VoiceDrop app. The keyboard is a thin text-insertion client. See the privacy policy for the full breakdown.

Does my voice leave the device?

No. Transcription runs on Apple's Neural Engine using the Parakeet model, which was downloaded once to your device during onboarding. After that, VoiceDrop makes no network requests at all. No audio is uploaded. No transcripts are uploaded. No usage analytics are collected.

Why is the first recording so slow?

The Parakeet model (~600 MB) downloads from Hugging Face the first time you open the app. After that, it lives on your device and loads in 15–20 seconds on a cold app start. While Parakeet is loading, VoiceDrop falls back to Apple Speech (lower quality but instant). Once Parakeet is warm, it's ~70× realtime — a one-second clip transcribes in under 15 ms.

How do I disable auto-stop on silence?

VoiceDrop → Settings tab → Silence detection. Toggle off. In-app recordings then require you to tap the mic button to stop. (Action Button and keyboard flows still auto-stop — they run without a visible stop button, so silence is how they know you're done.)

How do I uninstall everything?

Delete the app like any other iOS app. All transcripts, model cache, settings, and shared storage are cleared on uninstall. Nothing of yours remains on any server (because nothing was ever there).

Which languages work?

Parakeet TDT v3 supports 25 languages natively. English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Polish, Ukrainian, Romanian, Dutch, Swedish, Danish, Finnish, Norwegian, Czech, Greek, Hungarian, Turkish, Hebrew, Arabic, Chinese (Mandarin), Japanese, Korean, Hindi. In Auto mode, Parakeet detects the language per-recording. You can also pin a specific language in Settings → Language for better accuracy on less-common speech.

The transcript is wrong. What can I do?

During the keyboard flow, tap the Try again button on the done state. That discards the current transcript and starts a fresh recording, without returning to your messaging app. If a specific word is consistently wrong (your name, a place, technical jargon), email the developer — custom vocabulary is planned for a future Pro tier.

Reporting a bug

Any of these are worth a quick email:

  • Crashes or freezes
  • Transcription that's consistently wrong for a specific phrase
  • Keyboard doesn't insert text after you return to the host app
  • UI that's confusing or requires guessing
  • Battery drain during long recordings

If you reach the app's Settings tab, tap Send feedback and it'll pre-fill your build number, device model, and language mode so replies don't need back-and-forth. Nothing about what you dictated is ever included.
